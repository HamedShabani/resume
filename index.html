Welcome to my page!

I am passionate about the underlying mechanism of behavior in animals with small nervous systems and also developing new technologies by which we can manipulate and image the activity of these neural circuits. I have a background in Electrical Engineering and currently I am a PhD student in the Experimental Retinal Prosthetics lab at the Institute for Ophthalmic Research, University of Tübingen.

The aim of my PhD project is to find a novel way by which we can preferentially target different neuronal pathways in retina. To this end we use a wide range of visual stimuli plus Electrical noise stimulation to cluster the retinal Ganglion cells and estimate the electrical profile of each type. If you like to know more about my work please check this article out.

Before starting my PhD I worked as a research assistant at IPM. During this period I worked on human EEG signals and also electrophysiological data of primates.
